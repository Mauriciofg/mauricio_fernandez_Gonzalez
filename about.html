I pursued a Bachelor's degree in Biology at the Universidad Católica de Valparaíso. Subsequently, I conducted my thesis in the Biodiversity Laboratory at the University of Chile, where I analyzed the transgenerational response of Daphnia magna to copper exposure, assessing effects on body size and metabolic rate. Following this, I completed a Ph.D. in Integrative Genomics at the Universidad Mayor, focusing on the transcriptional response of genes to cadmium exposure, particularly in terms of energy allocation for growth at the genetic level. Additionally, I have collaborated with research groups involved in Cancer, Immunology, and Public Health research.

I am interested in how growth affects organism performance and the relationship between resource allocation control to this trait and growth-related diseases, such as cancer. My technical approach to these problems is through computer science and statistics. While my scientific approach to these issues aligns more with scientific thinking, with the phenomenon of life (Maturana and Varela) as my basis for scientific exploration.
